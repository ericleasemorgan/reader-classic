ELM was here. The Distant Reader is a tool for reading.

Intended for anybody who reads, Distant Reader takes text as input, does analysis against it, and outputs sets of structured data called “study carrels”, which students, researchers, or scholars can use to read both closely and at a distance.

Embrace information overload. Use the Distant Reader.

Working with Carrels

  • Browse Library of Carrels

    Peruse the more than 2,000 previously created study carrels, and learn the size and scope of content study carrels can contain.

  • Create a Carrel from Your Data

    Create study carrels based on a number of different types of input, including but not limited to: a URL, a list of URLs, a file, a zip file containing many files, HathiTrust collection files, or bioRxiv metadata files.

  • Create a Carrel from Project Gutenberg

    Study carrels can be created from a local subset of the venerable Project Gutenberg which is strong on English literature, American literature, and Western philosophy. Search the index, limit the results to items of interest -- such as the complete works of a given author or all the works on a given subject -- and create a carrel based on the results.

  • Create a Carrel from CORD-19 Data

    Very similar to the Gutenberg service outline above, you can create study carrels based on the content of an index of more than 500,000 scholarly journal articles on the topic of COVID-19. Search the index. Refine the results. Submit them to the Reader for processing. The results will include the original articles, their plain text transformation, sets of structured data used to describe the collection, and summaries of the whole.
