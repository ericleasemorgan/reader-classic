The Distant Reader is a tool for reading.

Intended for anybody who reads, Distant Reader takes text as input, does analysis against it, and outputs sets of structured data called “study carrels”, which students, researchers, or scholars can use to read both closely and at a distance.

Embrace information overload. Use the Distant Reader.

Working with Carrels

  • Browse Library of Carrels

    Browse the library of carrels you've created and those created and publicly shared by others.

  • Create a Carrel from Your Data

    Create a new carrel from a range of file types, including DOC/DOCX, PPT/PPTX, PDF, TXT, CSV, HTML and XML, and even URLs.

  • Create a Carrel from Project Gutenberg

    Create a carrel from the collection's approximately 65,000 freely available transcribed electronic texts. Its scope leans towards the Western canon, and it includes the vast majority of “classic” texts any high school or college student might want/need to read.

  • Create a Carrel from CORD-19 Data

    Create a carrel from a cache of more than 100,000 scholarly and scientific journal articles on the topic of COVID-19.
