Anybody with a Web browser and an Internet connection may use the Reader for free. To save your carrel, you need only login with a free ORCID ID, a Google ID, or your institutional credentials.

Currently, the Distant Reader will only process English-language texts. However, if there is enough demand, additional languages may be possible in future.

The amount of text the Reader can process varies significantly. For example, it can accept as little as a single file the size of a journal article. The Reader can also process an entire blog, hundreds of URLs, hundreds of books, or thousands of journal articles. The Reader's limitations are less about the amount of data given and more about one's ability to articulate the content that goes into a carrel.

You may share your carrels with others, only if it does not contain copyrighted materials. Since study carrels are computer platform- and network-independent, you can download your study carrel, put the result on your own website, and provide access to it from there.

You can also use the bioRxiv Archive and Hathi Trust collections.

  • Hathi Trust — is collection of millions of digitized texts originating from academic libraries. For items in the collection which are in the public domain, all are available in both PDF and OCR ("optical character recognition") formats. About 25% of the 'Trust's collection are in the public domain.
  • bioRxiv — as a collection of biology-related journal articles which have yet to be formally published, bioRxiv represents a kind of pre-print server of biology literature.
